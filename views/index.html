Welcome

Hello and welcome to Companies House!

To give you the best possible start on your Companies House Experience journey, we’ve created Key - our new tool that’s designed to be a one-stop shop for all the essential knowledge you need as a new starter in the organisation and beyond.

It provides useful information and resources, such as how to use the clocking system, how to set up for remote working and which support networks are available to you.

Key will be available to you for as long as you’re with us, meaning you’ll have access to all the key information you need, whenever you need it.

Companies House at a glance



Who are we?

Companies House is the UK’s registrar of companies. We're an executive agency, sponsored by the Department for Business, Energy and Industrial Strategy (BEIS).

What do we do?

Our main functions are to:

  • incorporate and dissolve limited companies
  • examine and store company information
  • make this information available to the public

What it’s like to work at Companies House

At the moment, almost everyone is working remotely. When we do return to the office, there’s free parking, an onsite gym, cafeteria and kitchen facilities.

There are several networks you can join that help you stay connected to people and get support when you need it.

You also get a highly competitive 30 days’ annual leave, as well as bank holidays, plus an additional day’s leave for the Queen’s birthday!

Office culture

At Companies House, you’re encouraged to bring your ‘whole self’ to work. There is no dress code, so whether you want to wear a shirt and tie or hoodie and jeans, that’s entirely up to you.

We’re adopting ‘smarter ways of working’, which is about flexibility, resilience and productivity. Examples of this include using new technologies, hot-desking and flexible working.

Flexi time

Companies House uses a flexible working system. You are expected to work 37 hours per week, which is around 7 hours 20 per day (based on full time hours). However, you can usually work these hours in any pattern between 6am and 8pm.

You can also build up extra flexi time across the week, so if you work over your contracted hours, you can take this as flexi leave when needed.

Volunteering

As an organisation, we want to do our bit to help out in the local community. That’s why we encourage you to volunteer for 3 days per year, as part of your working hours.
